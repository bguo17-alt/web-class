WHAT IT IS

Matcha is a collection of finely grounded green tea leaves. It has a green color and has a strong umami flavor that is derived from the amino acids within tea leaves. It absorbs moisture easily and can be discolored by light. Therefore it is optimal to avoid storing matcha in high temperatures and humidity to retain flavor and color.

ORIGINS

The history of matcha can be traced back to China during the Tang Dynasty (7th-10th centuries). During this time period, green tea harvests were compressed into bricks and turned into powder for easier transport. Japanese monks who studied Buddhism in China then brought the preperation of powdered green tea to Japan, which slowly flourished into the Japanese tea ceremony.
TYPES OF MATCHA GRADES

CEREMONIAL

Ceremonial Matcha is the highest quality grade. Characterized by a vibrant green color and fine, smooth texture, it is made to be served on its own whisked in hot water without any milk or flavoring. It is also more concentrated allowing you to use less powder to achieve a rich taste.

CAFE

Cafe Matcha is a subset of culinary matcha that is mainly used in cafes for drinks. While it features a more bitter and earthier flavor than ceremonial matcha, it is generally less expensive than ceremonial matcha, making it optimal for daily consumption.

CULINARY

Culinary Matcha is not as fine as other grades and is more bitter in flavor. It is used for large-scale brewing or baking, and is meant to be mixed with other ingredients in recpies where matcha is not the main star.
